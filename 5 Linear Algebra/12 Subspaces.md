### subspace
- nonempty subset of vector space that itself equal vector space with the same operations

---
### subspace formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal W \le \mathcal V \iff (\emptyset \ne \mathcal W  \subset \mathcal V) \land (\vec w_{1} + \vec w_{2} \in \mathcal W) \land (c\vec w \in \mathcal W) \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space} \\
\vec w = \text{vector} \\
c = \text{scalar}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### subspace example
- solution set of homogeneous linear ode
- lower triangular matrices
- upper triangular matrices
- diagonal matrices
- symmetric matrices
- skew symmetric matrices

---
### nonsubspace example
- solution set of nonhomogeneous linear system
- nonproportional linear relationship
- unit circle
- nonsingular matrices
- singular matrices
- RREF matrices

---
### trivial subspace
- smallest possible subspace whose single element equal zero vector

---
### trivial subspace formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal W = \{\vec 0\} \\
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### single element subspace
- linear combination form line through the origin

---
### two element subspace
- linear combination form plane through the origin

---
### three element subspace
- linear combination form space through the origin

---
### subspace zero property
- zero vector equal element of every subspace

---
### subspace zero property formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mathcal W \le\mathcal V \iff \vec 0 \in \mathcal W \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace} \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space} 
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### subspace intersection property
- intersection of subspace(s) equal subspace

---
### subspace intersection property formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\forall i \in I: \mathcal W_{i} \le \mathcal V \implies \bigcap_{i \in I} \mathcal W_{i} \le \mathcal V \\
I = \text{index set} \\
\mathcal W = \text{subspace}  \\
\mathcal V = \text{vector space} 
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### subspace eigenspace property
- eigenspace equal subspace of $n$-dimensional real numbers

---
### subspace eigenspace property formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
E_{\lambda} \le \mathbb R^n \\
E_{\lambda} = \text{eigenspace} \\
\lambda = \text{eigenvalue} 
\end{aligned}
$$
