### dynamics
- study of how forces affect the motion of objects

---
### force
- vector quantity of push or pull on object
---
### scalar component formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
F_{x} = F \cos (\theta) \\
F_{y} = F \sin (\theta) \\
F = \text{force} \\
\theta = \text{angle between vectors}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### force magnitude formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
F = \sqrt{F_{x}^2 + F_{y}^2} \\
F_{x} = \text{x scalar component} \\
F_{y} = \text{y scalar component}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### force direction formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\theta = \arctan(\frac{F_{y}}{F_{x}}) \\
F_{y} = \text{y scalar component} \\
F_{x} = \text{x scalar component}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### net external force
- vector sum of all outside forces acting on object
---
### net external force formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec F_{net} = \sum_{i} \vec F_{i} \\
F = \text{force}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### normal force
- perpendicular force by surface on object

---
### tension force
- pulling force by string on object

---
### weight force
- downward force by gravity on object

---
### friction force
- resistive force by surface on object

---
### spring force
- restoring force by spring on object

---
### contact force
- normal force
- tension force
- friction force
- spring force

---
### non contact force
- weight force
- electric force

---
### free body diagram
- draw body as the origin of coordinate system
- sketch all forces acting on the body as vectors
- resolve all vectors into scalar components
- formulate newtons 2nd law for all scalar components
- equilibrium equal $\sum F = 0$ 
- non equilibrium equal $\sum F = ma$ 
---
### newtons first law
- object at rest remains at rest or in motion with constant velocity unless net external force act on object

---
### newtons 1st formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec F_{net} = 0 \implies \frac{d\vec v}{dt} = 0 \\
F = \text{force} \\
v = \text{velocity} \\
t = \text{time}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### newtons second law
- acceleration dependent mass and amount of net external force acting on mass

---
### newtons 2nd formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec F_{net} = m\vec a =
\begin{cases}
F_{x} = ma_{x} \\
F_{y} = ma_{y}
\end{cases} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
a = \text{acceleration} \\
F_{x} = \text{x scalar component} \\
F_{y} = \text{y scalar component}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### newtons third law
- for every action force there exists equal and opposite reaction force
- equal magnitude and opposite direction

---
### newtons 3rd formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec F_{AB} = -\vec F_{BA} \\
F = \text{force}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### action reaction pair
- pair of equal and opposite forces resulting from the interaction of two objects
