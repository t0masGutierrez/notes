### momentum
- difficulty of stopping or changing direction of motion

---
### momentum formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec p = m\vec v \\
m = \text{mass} \\ 
v = \text{velocity}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### newtons second law
- acceleration dependent mass and net external force acting on mass

---
### newtons 2nd formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec F_{net} = \frac{d\vec p}{dt} =
\begin{cases}
F_{x} = p_{x} \\
F_{y} = p_{y}
\end{cases} \\
p = \text{momentum} \\
t = \text{time} \\
F_{x} = \text{x scalar component} \\
F_{y} = \text{y scalar component}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### impulse
- measure of net external force over time
---
### impulse formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec J = \sum F_{avg} \Delta t = \int_{t_{1}}^{t_{2}} \vec F_{net} \cdot d \vec t \\
F_{avg} = \text{average force} \\
F_{net} = \text{net force} \\
t = \text{time}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### impulse momentum
- impulse equal change of momentum

---
### impulse momentum formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec J = \Delta \vec p = m \Delta \vec v \\
p = \text{momentum} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
v = \text{velocity}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### impulse momentum versus work energy
- net force dependent time equal impulse momentum
- net force dependent position equal work energy

---
### momentum conservation
- zero net external force equal zero change of momentum

---
### momentum conservation formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec p_{i} = \vec p_{f} \\
p = \text{momentum}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### collision
- kinetic energy remains the same or decreases and momentum remains the same

---
### explosion
- kinetic energy increases
- single object break apart

---
### elastic collision
- kinetic energy remains the same
- multiple objects bounce off 

---
### inelastic collision
- kinetic energy decreases
- multiple objects bounce off

---
### complete inelastic collision
- kinetic energy equal zero
- multiple objects stick on

---
### center of mass
- point inside object that acts as the location of all mass

---
### center of mass formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec r_{cm} = \frac{\sum_{i} m_{i} \vec r_{i}}{M} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
r = \text{position} \\
M = \text{system mass}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### center of mass acceleration
- center of mass acceleration dependent mass and net external force acting on mass

---
### center of mass acceleration formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec a_{cm} = \frac{\sum_{i} m_{i} \vec a_{i}}{M} \\
\vec F = M\vec a_{cm} \\
m = \text{point mass} \\
a = \text{acceleration} \\
M = \text{system mass}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### center of mass velocity
- center of mass velocity dependent mass and momentum acting on mass

---
### center of mass velocity formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec v_{cm} = \frac{\sum_{i} m_{i} \vec v_{i}}{M} \\
\vec p = M\vec v_{cm} \\
m = \text{point mass} \\
v = \text{velocity} \\
M = \text{system mass}
\end{aligned}
$$
