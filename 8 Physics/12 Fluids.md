### solid
- definite shape
- definite volume
- low compressibility
- vibrational motion

---
### liquid
- indefinite shape
- definite volume
- moderate compressibility
- translational motion

---
### gas
- indefinite shape
- indefinite volume
- high compressibility
- random motion

---
### fluid
- liquid or gas

---
### volume
- amount of three dimensional space within object
---
### volume formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{cube} = lwh \\
\text{sphere} = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3 \\
\text{cyclinder} = \pi r^2h
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### calculate volume
- submerge object into liquid
- measure volume displacement

---
### density
- measure of compactness
---
### density formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\rho = \frac{m}{V} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
V = \text{volume}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### pressure
- measure of local force
---
### pressure formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
p = \frac{F\perp}{A} \\
F = \text{force} \\
A = \text{area}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### atmospheric pressure
- weight of air column
---
### atmospheric pressure formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{1 atm} = \text{101325 Pa}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### hydrostatic pressure
- weight of fluid column
---
### hydrostatic pressure formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
p = p_{0} + \rho gy \\
p_{0} = \text{surface pressure} \\
\rho = \text{density} \\
g = 9.8 \\
y = \text{position}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### gauge pressure
- measure of pressure relative atmospheric pressure

---
### gauge pressure formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{g} = p_{abs} - p_{atm} \\
p_{abs} = \text{absolute pressure} \\
p_{atm} = \text{atmospheric pressure} \\
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### manometer
- measure pressure inside container by comparing height of fluid column
---
### pascal principle
- uniform distribution of pressure change inside container
---
### pascal formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}} = \frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}} \\
F = \text{force} \\
A = \text{area}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### archimedes principle
- buoyant force equal weight of fluid displacement

---
### buoyant force
- upward force by fluid on object inside fluid via pressure imbalance between top and bottom
---
### buoyant force formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
F_{b} = \rho_{f} V_fg \\
\rho_{f} = \text{fluid density} \\
V_{f} = \text{fluid volume} \\
g = 9.8
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### calculate buoyant force
- buoyant force greater object weight cause floating
- buoyant force less object weight cause sinking
- buoyant force equal object weight cause suspension

---
### apparent weight
- object inside fluid appear less heavy due to buoyant force

---
### apparent weight formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
w_{app} =w - F_{b} \\
w = \text{weight} \\
F_{b} = \text{buoyant force}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### volume flow rate
- amount of fluid flow through cross section over time
---
### volume flow rate formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\frac{dV}{dt} = A v \\
A = \text{area} \\
v = \text{velocity}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### incompressible fluid
- constant density aka mass conservation
---
### continuity formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
A_{1} v_{1} = A_{2} v_{2} \\
A = \text{area} \\
v = \text{velocity}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### viscous fluid
- internal friction of fluid cause velocity change along solid boundary
---
### viscosity formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\mu = \frac{Fy}{Av} \\
F = \text{force} \\
y = \text{position} \\
A = \text{area} \\
v = \text{velocity}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### ideal fluid
- constant density and zero viscosity

---
### bernoullis principle
- for ideal fluids there exists inverse relationship between pressure and velocity

---
### bernoulli formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
p_{1} + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_{1}^2 + \rho gy_{1} = p_{2} + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_{2}^2 + \rho gy_{2} \\
p = \text{pressure} \\
\rho = \text{density} \\
v = \text{velocity} \\
g = 9.8 \\
y = \text{position}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### special bernoulli formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\text{static flow} = p_{1} + \rho gy_{1} = p_{2} + \rho gy_{2} \\
\text{horizontal flow} = p_{1} + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_{1}^2 = p_{2} + \frac{1}{2}\rho v_{2}^2 \\
p = \text{pressure} \\
\rho = \text{density} \\
g = 9.8 \\
y = \text{position} \\
v = \text{velocity} 
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### drag force
- resistive force by fluid on object

---
### drag force formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
\vec F_{d} = \frac{1}{2}\rho A \vec v^2C_{d} \\
\rho = \text{density} \\
A = \text{area} \\
v = \text{velocity} \\
C_{d} = \text{drag coefficient}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### terminal velocity
- free falling object with constant velocity

---
### terminal velocity formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
m \vec g = \vec F_{d} \implies \vec v = \sqrt{\frac{2m \vec g}{\rho C_dA}} \\
m = \text{mass} \\
g = \text{9.8} \\
F_{d} = \text{drag force} \\
v = \text{velocity} \\
\rho = \text{density} \\
C_{d} = \text{drag coefficient} \\
A = \text{area}
\end{aligned}
$$
