### variable
- symbol representing quantity that can vary

---
### variable formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
x, y, a, b, n, k
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### constant
- symbol representing quantity that cannot vary

---
### constant formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
3, -5, \frac{1}{2}, 0.6, \pi
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### coefficient
- constant multiplying variable

---
### coefficient formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
ax \\
a = \text{coefficient} \\
x = \text{variable}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### algebraic expression
- mathematical statement composed of numbers, variables, and operations

---
### algebraic expression formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
ax + b \\
a = \text{coefficient} \\
x = \text{variable} \\
b = \text{constant} 
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### term
- piece of algebraic expression between addition or subtraction

---
### term formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
ax + by + c \implies ax, by, c \\
a, b = \text{coefficient} \\
x, y = \text{variable} \\
c = \text{constant}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### like term
- term(s) with the same variable and exponent

---
### like term formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
ax^n + bx^n \implies (a+b)x^n 
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### operation
- mathematical action performed on numbers or algebraic expressions

---
### operation formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
(+) = \text{addition} \\
(-) = \text{subtraction} \\
(\cdot ) = \text{multiplication} \\
(\div) = \text{division} \\
(^n) = \text{exponentiation}
\end{aligned}
$$

---
### order of operations
- PEMDAS and left-to-right

---
### order of operations formula
$$
\begin{aligned}
1. \text{ parenthesis} \\
2. \text{ exponents} \\
3. \text{ multiplication} \\
4. \text{ division} \\
5. \text{ addition} \\
6. \text{ subtraction} \\
\end{aligned}
$$
